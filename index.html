Privacy Policy for Gogo Spoon

Effective date: July 09, 2026

Gogo Spoon ("the app") is developed by Minkyung Cho ("we", "us"). This Privacy Policy explains what information the app accesses, how it's used, and your choices.

Information the app accesses

Location. When you search for a restaurant, the app asks for your device's location (foreground only — we never request or use "Always" background location access) to help rank search results by proximity. This location is sent to Apple's Maps Server API as part of the search request (see "Third-party services" below). We do not store your location on any server we control, and we do not track your location history. The "Nearby" button does not use your location through the app at all — it simply opens your device's Maps app directly.

You can deny or revoke location access at any time in your device's Settings. The app will still work without it, except that proximity-based search ranking won't be available.

Restaurant data you create. The app lets you save restaurants (name, address, your personal star rating, visit history, and notes on what you ordered). This data is stored only on your device, using local app storage. We do not upload it to any server, and we do not have access to it. If you delete the app, this data is permanently deleted along with it — there is currently no cloud backup or account system.

Search queries. When you type a restaurant name to search, that text (and, if available, your location as a proximity hint) is sent to Apple's Maps Server API to retrieve matching results. We do not store your search history ourselves.

Third-party services

The app uses the Apple Maps Server API to search for restaurants and show basic details (name, address). We operate a small server-side component whose only job is authenticating with this API on the app's behalf — it does not receive or store your location, search terms, or restaurant data; those go directly from your device to Apple. This is subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.

Depending on your settings, tapping a restaurant's name opens either a Google Maps search or a Yelp search for more details, and tapping the map icon opens either Apple Maps or Google Maps for directions. These simply open a link in the app you chose — only the restaurant's name and address are included in that link. Use of those apps is subject to their own privacy policies (Google, Yelp).

What we don't do

  • We don't collect your name, email, or any account information — the app has no login or user accounts.
  • We don't use analytics or advertising SDKs.
  • We don't sell or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes.
  • We don't operate any database of our own — all your saved data lives only on your device.

Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes (for example, if we add cloud sync or accounts in the future), we'll update this page and revise the effective date above.
